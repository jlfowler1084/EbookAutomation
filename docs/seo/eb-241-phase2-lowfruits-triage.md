# EB-241 Phase 2 — Unit 9 LowFruits triage synthesis

## TL;DR — three findings that reshape Phase 3

1. **Every one of the 40 SERP-analyzed seeds is winnable.** SERP scores are 1-3 across the board (LowFruits' scale tops at 5; 1-3 = "easy to very easy"). 25th percentile is 1.75. The leafbind-niche corner of the SEO landscape is genuinely undefended — confirming Phase 1's read.

2. **The Related-queries expansion exposed Phase 1's biggest miss.** LowFruits surfaced 173 Related queries (semantic neighbors of our seeds), and 65 of them have measurable volume — including a single root query at **90,500 monthly searches** that Phase 1's Semrush run never touched. A second LowFruits pass against the Related-tab top-20 is the highest-leverage Phase 3a action item.

3. **The weakness signal is not coming from forums.** Our enabling "Forums as weak spots" toggle barely fired — `# UGC/Forums (Top 10)` is 0 for almost every analyzed keyword. The weakness instead comes from very low domain authority results (`Lowest DA (Top 10)` of 1-20 for most entries) and the custom-highlighted domains (YouTube, Play Store, App Store, docgenie.co.uk). This is a finer-grained map of where leafbind can compete than Phase 1's audit could produce.

## Appendix B — LowFruits column reference

Key columns from the 28-column export, for future reference:

- **SERP Score** — composite difficulty score (1-5 scale, lower = easier)
- **# Weak Spots (Top 10)** — count of weakness signals in top 10 results
- **# Weak Spots (Top 3)** — same for top 3 (more important — top 3 capture ~75% of clicks)
- **# UGC / Forums (Top 10)** — count of Reddit/Quora/forum results in top 10
- **Lowest DA (Top 10)** — lowest domain authority among top 10 (lower = more winnable for new domains)
- **Allintitle / Vol** — competition ratio (lower = less competition relative to demand)
- **Featured Snippet** — whether a featured snippet is showing (lowers click-through)
- **Image / Video (Position)** — image/video pack positions (lowers click-through if high)
- **Tab** — origin: suggestion (our seed), question (PAA), or related
